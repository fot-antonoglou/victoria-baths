History

The disgner of the baths was Henry Price who created the department of the City Architect of 1902 ( 4 years before Victoria Baths were opened to the public). The lord Mayor of Manchester (Cllr Abid Latif Chohan) had named the baths the name they have now, and he described the baths as a "water palace". The baths had provided leisure and essential facilities ( for 83 years). A laundry a Turkish bath were housed there along with a sauna and three swimming pools. The main swimming pool was floored (in winter months) to hold some dances. In 1952, the baths had instaled the first public jacuzzi in Englad. A great deal of money (in the desighn and constructionof the baths). At that time, Manchester was having one of the wealthiest munical coffers in the world. The Manchester City Council closed the baths in 1993. The friends of Victoria Baths had formed and began to investigating the possibility of running Victoria Baths (indepedently). The work to a prevent further deterionation of the building had started in 1998.

Restoration

In September 2003, tha baths won the first series of the BBC Restoration programme. It was awarded with 3.4 million pounds from the Hertage Lottery Fund. To help celebrate the win, the prince of Wales visited the baths, one month later.In September 2005, the baths had recieved the final plan to start the restoration process. In 2006, the Gala pool was fulled in 13 years time for the first time, as part of an event number to mark the centenary of the building's opening. On Monday 19th September 2007, the first work of the restoration began and it was complete in 2008. In 2011, the baths were used as a location of filming. On the 16th of April 2017, the baths were reopened again, only for an acid dance party hosted by a room of Boiler.
